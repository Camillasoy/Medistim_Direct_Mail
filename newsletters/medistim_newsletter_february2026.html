Publication Highlight

Learning Curve of Cardiac Surgery Residents in Transit-Time Flow Measurement and High-Resolution Epicardial Ultrasonography During Coronary Surgery

Cammertoni et al.

Learning curve of cardiac surgery residents in transit-time flow measurement and high-resolution epicardial ultrasonography during coronary surgery. J Clin Med. 2026;15(2):620. doi:10.3390/jcm15020620

Cammertoni and Di Giammarco have challenged the perception of a steep learning process to incorporate TTFM and HFUS during CABG. They found that just 3 cases and 7 anastomoses were sufficient training and concluded:

"Mastery of basic TTFM and HFUS skills requires only a few cases and anastomoses, demonstrating a short and attainable learning curve."

They support the routine use of intraoperative graft verification techniques in all CABG procedures.

Keywords: CABG, HFUS, TTFM; Di Giammarco

High-resolution epicardial ultrasound (HRUS) scoring system. After completion of each distal anastomosis, the resident performed HRUS acquisition.

Customer Webinars

Making Grafts Count: TTFM in Contemporary CABG

On January 24, 2026, the International Society for Coronary Artery Surgery (ISCAS) hosted the webinar Making Grafts Count: Transit-Time Flow Measurement (TTFM) in Contemporary CABG. Leading experts, including John Puskas, Torsten Doenst, Gianluca Torregrossa, and Mojgan Laali, shared practical guidance on interpreting flow measurements and optimising intraoperative decision-making in CABG.

The recording underscores the strong clinical evidence supporting TTFM and explores why adoption remains inconsistent despite guideline recommendations — making it a valuable resource for surgeons looking to evaluate or refine their use of TTFM in daily practice.
