■ Publication Highlight

Intraoperative Graft Flow Predicts Early Bypass Outcomes in Chronic Limb-Threatening Ischemia With Influence of Pedal Artery Anatomy

Kikuchi et al. · Department of Vascular Surgery, Asahikawa Medical University, Japan

This study from the PATENT site in Asahikawa, led by Professor Azuma, evaluates the utility of the Medistim VeriQ™ system, incorporating the Inframalleolar/Pedal Descriptor (IMPD) classification to stratify pedal arterial complexity based on the Global Vascular Guidelines.

Data from 165 patients was analyzed, finding that intraoperative graft flow measurement provides meaningful prognostic information in para- and inframalleolar bypass for CLTI. Flow thresholds (<25 and <40 mL/min) and IMPD classification help identify high-risk anatomy and guide intraoperative decisions and postoperative surveillance.

■ Did you miss our last Direct Mail?

What you can't see can fail your graft.
 

In CABG, roughly 4% of target coronary arteries are intramural, buried in the myocardium and invisible to the naked eye. When an LAD sits 6mm deep, preoperative angiography can map the coronary tree, but won't tell you how deep the target lies beneath the epicardial surface. HFUS changes that, giving surgeons precise localization of the anastomotic site before incision.

■ New DASH Structure

Now With Easier Access to Materials

We have updated the DASH folder structure to make it more intuitive and easier to navigate. The structure is now organized around 4 clear sections:

01

Sales & Marketing

All commercial and promotional resources are now grouped.

02

Clinical & Scientific

Clinical studies, guidelines, application materials, and patient cases.

03

Product Documentation

User manuals, IFUs, quick guides, and regulatory documents.

Training & Education

EduQ remains our primary training platform. Additional materials complement EduQ for ongoing learning.

Our goal is to make DASH faster and more intuitive to use. This is a first step, and we will continue refining the structure as we go. If you have feedback or find something difficult to locate, please let us know.
