Publication Highlight

Intraoperative Graft Flow Predicts Early Bypass Outcomes in Chronic Limb-Threatening Ischaemia With Influence of Pedal Artery Anatomy

Kikuchi et al.

Department of Vascular Surgery, Asahikawa Medical University, Japan. J Vasc Surg Vasc Insights. 2026. doi:10.1016/j.jvsvi.2026.00053

This study from the PATENT site in Asahikawa, led by Professor Azuma, evaluates the utility of the Medistim VeriQ™ system in para- and inframalleolar bypass for chronic limb-threatening ischaemia (CLTI). The Inframalleolar/Pedal Descriptor (IMPD) classification was used to stratify pedal arterial complexity based on the Global Vascular Guidelines.

Analysing data from 165 patients, the authors found that intraoperative graft flow measurement provides meaningful prognostic information. Flow thresholds of <25 and <40 mL/min, combined with IMPD classification, help identify high-risk anatomy and guide both intraoperative decisions and postoperative surveillance.

Did You Miss Our Last Direct Mail?

What You Can't See Can Fail Your Graft

In CABG, roughly 4% of target coronary arteries are intramural — buried in the myocardium and invisible to the naked eye. When an LAD sits 6 mm deep, preoperative angiography can map the coronary tree, but won't tell you how deep the target lies beneath the epicardial surface.

HFUS changes that — giving surgeons precise localisation of the anastomotic site before incision.

New DASH Structure

Now With Easier Access to Materials

We have updated the DASH folder structure to make it more intuitive and easier to navigate. The structure is now organised around four clear sections:

01 Sales & Marketing — All commercial and promotional resources grouped in one place.

02 Clinical & Scientific — Clinical studies, guidelines, application materials, and patient cases to support scientific dialogue.

03 Product Documentation — User manuals, IFUs, quick guides, and regulatory documents in one dedicated location.

04 Training & Education — EduQ remains the primary training platform; materials here complement EduQ and support ongoing team learning, including the STIM Selling refresher, scenario video, and conversation guide.

Our goal is to make DASH faster and more intuitive to use. This is a first step and we will continue refining the structure as we go. If you have feedback or find something difficult to locate, please let us know.
