When an LAD hides 6 mm deep in the myocardium, palpation and preoperative imaging won't find it. High-frequency ultrasound will.

~ 4 %

of target coronary arteries are intramural — embedded in myocardium and invisible to the naked eye at the time of anastomosis.

The Problem

An intramural LAD is one of the more demanding scenarios in CABG. The vessel is there, but it's hidden.

Preoperative angiography maps the coronary tree, but it doesn't tell you how deep a target sits beneath the epicardial surface. When your LAD is tunneled into the myocardium, the anastomotic site requires precise localization before you can plan incision depth, avoid unnecessary tissue injury, and confirm vessel quality.

 

Without intraoperative imaging, you're relying on indirect cues and experience alone. High-frequency ultrasound (HFUS) changes that, giving surgeons precise localisation of the anastomotic site before incision depth is committed.

“Hayakawa et al. reported that 10 out of 89 consecutive patients had intramural coronary targets — a proportion significant enough to warrant a systematic imaging strategy in every CABG list.”
