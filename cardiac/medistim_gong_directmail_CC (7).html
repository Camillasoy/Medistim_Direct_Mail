The Case

Refractory ventricular fibrillation mid-CABG

A 65-year-old man underwent urgent off-pump CABG at Peking University First Hospital. The LIMA–LAD anastomosis and sequential SVG were completed without incident. During preparation of the proximal aortic anastomosis, the patient developed refractory ventricular fibrillation. Multiple defibrillations failed. Emergency CPB was established.

 

With ventricular function severely depressed, the surgical team measured TTFM using the Medistim MiraQ™, and the result was unambiguous: the LIMA–LAD graft was carrying flow in the wrong direction, retrograde at –15 mL/min. IABP pressure exceeded left radial pressure by 30–40 mmHg. Together, these findings confirmed critical proximal left subclavian artery stenosis causing acute Coronary–Subclavian Steal Syndrome (CSSS).

Intraoperative CSSS is one of the rarest complications in CABG. Without real-time flow data, this diagnosis would have remained invisible.

“TTFM provided unequivocal evidence of retrograde flow within the LIMA–LAD graft, directly establishing the presence of a steal phenomenon.”
